Virtual Bug (Spiking Neural Network)

The concept of a Braitenberg vehicle is manifested in the form of a virtual bug, created on Python using the Brian2 simulator package, to observe its validity. Specifically, a Braitenberg vehicle moves around based on its sensor inputs, which excite or inhibit its motors, or wheels, to create complex movement in an environment. Through extensive trial-run testing, a virtual bug was created that uses two pairs of neuronal sensors and two motor neurons (6 neurons total, Izhikevich model) to guide its locomotive behavior in navigating toward food while avoiding bug traps. The mechanism behind the resulting navigation algorithm is described in Fig 1c. Results demonstrate that the Braitenberg vehicle concept is effective in explaining complex behavior as a resultant of rather simple underlying circuits and show that arbitrary constants affecting sensitivity and strengths of neurons and synaptic connections are incredibly impactful on the resulting behavior, or locomotion, of the bug.

Hopfield Network

Figure 1 shows the four fundamental states (patterns) memorized by the Hopfield network for a 16-neuron model. When given a corrupted pattern (Figure 2), the most similar fundamental state is recovered and presented by activating the Hopfield network through applying the input vector X (corrupted pattern) to the calculated weight matrix and applying the sign function, which returns an element-wise indication of the sign of a number. Each fundamental pattern attracts unstable states (corrupted patterns) that have minimal error compared to the itself – the Hopfield network acts as an error correction network.

Project Overview

Figure 1. Optical Design in Zemax.The fundus camera optical design consists of three subsystems: a base collection arm (BLUE) that forms a sharp image of the retina onto the camera sensor using a Keplerian telescope, an illumination arm (RED) that introduces an LED at the iris image plane to brighten the field of image, and a fixation target arm (GREEN) introduced at the retinal image plane that allows the patients to stabilize their gaze. A working distance of 19mm was achieved.

Figure 2. Electrical Components used. An 800nm 32mW LED from ThorLabs was connected to the 5V pin for illumination with a current limiting resistor.
